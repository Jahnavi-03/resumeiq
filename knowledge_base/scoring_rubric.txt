ATS RESUME SCORING GUIDE
=========================
Based on industry research from Jobscan, Rezi, 
Enhancv, and resume.io

SECTION 1: WHAT ATS SCORE MEANS
There is no single official ATS scoring standard.
Different ATS systems score differently.
A score of 75 or above significantly improves 
interview chances according to Jobscan research.
A score of 80 or above is considered excellent
according to Enhancv and resume.io research.
99% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS systems.
Over 75% of large employers use ATS to filter 
candidates before human review.

SECTION 2: WHAT TO EVALUATE FOR SCORING
When scoring a resume against a job description
consider these factors in order of importance:

1. KEYWORD AND SKILLS MATCH (most important)
   - Are required skills from JD present in resume?
   - Are exact keywords from JD used in resume?
   - Are both full form and acronym included?
     Example: "Azure Data Factory (ADF)"
   - Are keywords in important sections?
     Summary and Work Experience carry most weight
   - Frequency matters: keyword used multiple times
     in relevant sections boosts match

2. EXPERIENCE RELEVANCE
   - Does experience level match JD requirement?
   - Are past roles relevant to target role?
   - Is there clear career progression?
   - Are there unexplained employment gaps?

3. BULLET POINT QUALITY
   - Do bullets start with strong action verbs?
   - Are achievements quantified with numbers?
   - Do bullets follow XYZ format?
   - Are bullets concise (under 2 lines)?

4. RESUME FORMAT AND PARSABILITY
   - Single column layout?
   - Standard section headers?
   - No tables, graphics, or columns?
   - Consistent date formatting?
   - Contact info in body not header/footer?

5. PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
   - Is there a summary?
   - Does it mention target role?
   - Does it include a quantified achievement?
   - No personal pronouns?

SECTION 3: SCORING GUIDANCE
Use your expertise as a senior FAANG recruiter
to assign a score from 0-100 based on overall
evaluation of the above factors.

Score interpretation:
90-100: Excellent — highly optimized, very likely 
        to pass ATS and impress human reviewer
75-89:  Good — solid match, likely to pass ATS
        with minor improvements needed
60-74:  Moderate — passes some ATS filters but
        missing important keywords or skills
40-59:  Weak — likely filtered by ATS, significant
        improvements needed
0-39:   Poor — major mismatch, fundamental 
        rewrite required

SECTION 4: WHAT TO ALWAYS INCLUDE IN FEEDBACK
1. ATS score (0-100) with one sentence explanation
2. Top 3 specific reasons for the score
3. Every required skill missing from resume
4. Every preferred skill missing from resume
5. Which exact JD keywords are missing
6. Top 3 bullet points that need improvement
7. Rewritten versions of those bullets in XYZ format
8. Top 3 specific actionable suggestions
9. Estimated score after suggested improvements

SECTION 5: IMPORTANT SCORING RULES
- Never give a perfect 100 — there is always room
  for improvement
- Never give below 10 — even a poor resume has
  some redeeming qualities
- Be honest but constructive — the goal is to help
  the candidate improve
- Base score on JD requirements not general standards
- A resume for wrong role should score low even if
  well written
- Quantify your reasoning — explain why you gave 
  that specific score
